## Overview

This capstone project explores how people evaluate AI-generated and human-written responses when the source of the answer is hidden.

To investigate this question, we conducted a blind survey involving 21 respondents and 12 questions covering topics such as leadership, career decisions, happiness, trust, failure, loneliness, and personal growth.

For each question, participants were presented with two anonymous responses: one written by a human and one generated by AI. Respondents were asked to select the answer they found more helpful, convincing, or meaningful without knowing its source.

The results showed that AI responses were preferred in 8 out of 12 questions (66.7%), particularly in topics requiring structure, objectivity, and practical guidance. Human responses, however, were preferred in emotionally sensitive topics involving failure, trust, and personal recovery.

The findings suggest that the effectiveness of a response depends less on who wrote it and more on the context in which it is used. Rather than competing with one another, AI and humans appear to serve different communication needs.
